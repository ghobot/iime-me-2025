Scaling Our Investment

Together with the Health Science Library, we are digitizing Plastinated Human Cadavers so students can access them anywhere at scale. These interactive models are part of a big update to our Anatomy Lab Manual.

Navigating Virtual Cadavers

Students will navigate scanned plastinated models on any device including in VR via Sketchfab. Understanding the 3D spatial relationships between structures, a traditional challenge of navigating cadavers, is improved using VR and AR.

Animated Anatomy

One difference of virtual simulations of anatomy over traditional cadavers is the opportunity to understand how structure informs function and physiology and disease. In this highly detailed simulation of the heart, students will see the organ's normal processes and the pathology of several heart diseases.

Simulation in VR

Practice high risk, low occurring situations.

Students will increasingly have new opportunities before meeting patients to practice their skills with realistic scenarios like this pediatric case in the Emergency room that includes varying levels of distraction.

Virtual Tour Orientation

Become more acclimated with your workspace.

With our expanding campus, understanding our spaces that feature new technology and diverse staff roles is now even more important. We are creating virtual tours of spaces our learners will visit, like this brand new surgery operating room in the new Kimmel Hospital.

Accessibilty to unique learning spaces.

We are offering learning spaces like the pathology lab to show students how anatomy and pathology are intrinsic to diagnosis. In this immersive video able to be viewed in VR, a pathologist examines this specimen and describes the likelihood of polyps being cancerous.
